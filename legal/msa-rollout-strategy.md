# MSA Rollout Strategy (Confidential)

Decision: [[decisions/2024-msa-isv-rollout|MSA + ISV Rollout]] | Customers: [[customers/agrisar/agrisar-overview|Agrisar]], [[customers/mana/mana-overview|Mana]], [[customers/agropeak/agropeak-overview|Agropeak]]

Moved from `decisions/2024-msa-isv-rollout.md` during vault sanitization.

## Rollout Strategy

Simultaneous rollout to all active customers:
- Agrisar
- Agropeak
- Mana

## Legal Counsel

**ALBM Excello Law**, represented by **Richard Ding**.
- Familiar with Dubai / DMCC legal landscape
- Handling bespoke MSA + ISV drafting

## Context — Agrisar Driver

Key driver was the Agrisar dispute: requests for DB schema access, third-party developer involvement, and pushback on CSV-only export policy exposed gaps in contractual clarity around IP ownership and usage rights.

MSA rollout specifically designed to codify IP boundaries and provide contractual basis for denying schema access requests.

## Pricing Concessions

- Agrisar receives a **12-month legacy pricing grace period** post-MSA signature as a negotiation concession
- Legal costs for bespoke MSA + ISV drafting through ALBM Excello

## Risks & Strategy

- Simultaneous rollout to all three customers is ambitious — any one customer pushing back delays the entire program
- Agrisar may use MSA negotiation as leverage to re-open DB access demands
- If any customer refuses to sign, Trackon must decide whether to continue service on informal terms (weakening the policy) or risk losing the customer
- Richard Ding / ALBM Excello familiar with Dubai / DMCC legal landscape
