# Canva Flyer Brief — SGGT Finalist 2026

**Output format:** 1080×1080 (Instagram/LinkedIn square) AND 1080×1920 (story/portrait) — the organizer will likely use both. Optionally also A4 landscape PDF for print.

---

## Design philosophy: less words, more feeling

A flyer should be **glanceable in 2 seconds** and **memorable for 2 minutes**. Print/social audiences don't read; they scan. Lead with:
1. An evocative image (a grain ship, wheat field, port silos, hands holding grain — emotional)
2. One bold headline (5-8 words max)
3. Logo prominent
4. QR code as the call-to-action
5. Minimal supporting text

Everything else can live in the LinkedIn caption, not on the flyer.

## Audience reality — WHO we're talking to

The flyer goes out to **IMEAA's 30,000+ agri-members, GAFTA/IGTC's 8,000+ trade members, and 63 IGC member countries.** That audience is overwhelmingly **SME-sized grain trading houses running on Excel + emails + WhatsApp** — priced out of ION/Brady's $500K stack, but quietly desperate for a real system.

Trackon sells BOTH:
- **TrackonAI** — the CTRM that replaces their Excel-and-emails workflow (the bigger audience, bigger pain)
- **TradingDocs.AI** — the AI compliance engine that prevents cargo refusals (sharper pain for fewer people)

The flyer headline should cover BOTH — or the bigger audience (Excel users) wins.

## Headline options (pick ONE — no more, no less)

| Headline | Voice | Primary pain addressed |
|---|---|---|
| ⭐ **"Your business runs on memory. It should run on a system."** | Brand-canonical, dual-product | Excel chaos · knowledge concentration · whole positioning |
| "Stop running grain trade on spreadsheets and luck." | Direct provocation | Excel chaos + cargo-refusal in one breath |
| "Your cargo won't get refused." | Direct promise (TradingDocs-only) | Document-compliance pain |
| "The trade OS we wished we had." | Founder voice | Both, soft |

**Recommendation: the first one.** Already your brand canon (TrackonAI marketing deck). Covers BOTH product stories. "Memory" hits an SME grain trader who watches their best ops person carry 200 spreadsheets in their head.

## Sub-headline (the credibility line)

⚠️ **Avoid: "Built by grain traders. For grain traders."** — reads as "we are competitors who will see your data."

✅ **Use one of:**

| Sub-headline | What it signals |
|---|---|
| ⭐ **"From inside the grain trade. Built for grain traders."** | Heritage + service. No conflict-of-interest read. |
| "20+ years inside the grain trade. Now on your side." | Same idea, more action-oriented |
| "Built by trading-systems engineers. Priced for grain traders, not for Cargill." | Adds the SME affordability hook |
| "Trading-systems engineers, by craft. From inside the grain trade, by experience." | Most explicit "we are software, not traders" |

## Image ideas (free sources)

| Source | Why |
|---|---|
| **Unsplash** — "grain ship", "wheat field", "port silos", "bulk carrier", "spreadsheet" | High-quality, free, commercial-use OK |
| **Pexels** — same searches | Backup |
| **Pixabay** — agricultural / port photos | Wider variety |

**Strongest image directions for THIS message (memory → system):**

1. **Strongest idea: a SPLIT visual** — left half a cluttered handwritten notebook or messy stack of papers (the "memory"), right half a clean tablet showing structured data (the "system"). Dramatic, instantly readable.
2. **An aerial view of port silos** — recognizable to anyone in trade, professional feel, suggests "infrastructure-grade system."
3. **A bulk carrier at sea (Panamax/Capesize)** — scale + global trade
4. **Wheat field at golden hour** — evocative, warm, friendly. Pair with cool blue overlay for tech-meets-tradition feel
5. **Hands holding wheat grains** — humanizes "by grain traders, for grain traders"

Avoid:
- Generic "data dashboard" images (corporate, sterile — the very thing we're saying we're NOT)
- Stock photos of people in suits in meetings (cliché)
- AI/abstract tech imagery (it's about grain, not AI hype)
- Excel spreadsheet screenshots on their own (too literal, depressing for the reader)

## Quick-start: Canva templates that would work

In Canva, search:
- **"Tech startup poster"** (1080×1080)
- **"Modern business flyer minimal"**
- **"LinkedIn post professional dark"**

Filter by:
- Aspect ratio: 1:1 (square) or 9:16 (story)
- Style: Minimal, Modern, Corporate
- Color: Pick one that allows dark navy + warm gold accents

## Brand spec (Canva color palette)

|---|---|---|
| Primary | `#506CEA` | Logo color accents, links |
| Dark navy | `#0F1628` | Background overlay on imagery |
| Warm gold | `#C8923B` | Highlight accent (sparingly) |
| Cream | `#FAF8F4` | Light card backgrounds |
| Soft white | `#FFFFFF` | Main text on dark backgrounds |

**Fonts:** Outfit (headings) + Raleway (body) — both available in Canva. If those aren't there: Montserrat (heading) + Inter (body) are reasonable substitutes.
