## Backstory

Back in 2021, Sedat was the type to tell Murat "just add a shipment tracking feature, it's very easy, I can do it myself." Classic client who sees software as nothing more than putting buttons on a screen. As a friend summarized: "For guys like this, the profession was never more than 'put a button, make it do this.' Now it's evolved into 'I can put that button myself, what's the big deal.'" — AI just gave this mindset the tools to act on it.

## Timeline

- **2021**: Mediterra evaluated Trackon as ERP solution. ~4 months of messaging and negotiation. Sedat would suggest features as if trivial ("just add shipment tracking, it's easy"). Ghosted without explanation.
- **2026-04-09**: Sedat reconnected on LinkedIn after seeing Trackon's MCP post. Exchanged pleasantries.
- **2026-04-10**: When asked if they found an ERP, Sedat replied: "Yes, I'm building it myself with Claude Opus."

Revealed he has also built software for a broker friend — he's becoming a micro-ISV powered by AI. Murat showcased TrackonAI capabilities (AI-powered invoice entry, document control, sanction analysis, shipping ETA tracking). Sedat was receptive. Murat floated a light collaboration/commission idea.

## Strategic Notes

- **Market signal**: A non-developer commodity trader is now confident enough to build his own ERP using AI (Claude Opus) — and is now building for others (broker friends). This validates that AI tools lower the barrier to both "build vs. buy" decisions AND the emergence of one-person software shops in commodity trading.
- **Sedat's own insight**: "herkes yazılımcı olacak ama satabilmek önemli" — building is commoditized, distribution/sales is the moat. This aligns with Trackon's advantage: domain expertise + established customer base.
- **Re-engagement opportunity**: Self-built ERPs typically hit scaling walls (multi-user, compliance, integrations, maintenance). Monitor for future re-engagement when complexity bites. Alternatively, Sedat could become a channel partner if his broker network grows.
- **Lesson**: The MCP post was the trigger for reconnection — content marketing works for staying top-of-mind even with lost prospects.
